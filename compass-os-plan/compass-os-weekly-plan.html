Compass OS — Delivery Plan

Week-by-week breakdown of the Stage 1 Statement of Work.

Compass Buyers Agency · 4 months · $50,000 + GST · Three streams: Web app · Bruce on WhatsApp · Agent space · By Roadmap Digital · Prepared for Nick & Chris

Companion document. The commercial spec lives in the Statement of Work. The SOW defines outcomes, milestones, acceptance gates, pricing, and risks. This document is the week-by-week build detail of how the SOW gets delivered. The two are designed to be read together.
What's in this document.
  • 16 weeks broken down ticket by ticket, grouped by Stream A (Web app), Stream B (Bruce on WhatsApp), Stream C (Agent space).
  • What Compass needs to do each week, in plain English. Most weeks: 30-min Monday catch-up plus a 5-min Friday Loom.
  • Three milestone gates (M1, M2, M3) with their acceptance criteria taken straight from the SOW.
  • The kick-off pack referenced in the SOW: every account, every artefact, every joint decision needed to start.

Who's building this

A small, focused team:

  • Daniel as Delivery Lead. Single point of contact. Plans the work, runs the Monday catch-ups, owns every milestone sign-off.
  • A local engineer working alongside Daniel on the build. Engineering depth and review on every code change before it reaches production.
  • A purpose-built agent stack running vetted skills refined across multiple prior projects. Handles drafting, scaffolding, and the long tail of build work.

The agents aren't the headline. They're the tools that let a small team deliver studio-scale throughput. What makes the work above standard is the craft layered on top: curated quality of inputs, meticulous output refinement, and human-reviewed integration. Compass deals with Daniel.

Risks and how we handle them

Same three risks as the SOW. Surfaced again here so the build team operates with them in mind every week.

Risk 01
WhatsApp transport reliability

Bruce's WhatsApp number can drop. Outbound can be rate-limited if too aggressive. The whole capture story leans on this working.

How we handle it. WhatsApp is the transport, not the source of truth. Every message mirrors into Supabase as an append-only event log. If Bruce drops, capture pauses, records survive. Outbound is rate-limited. Heartbeat monitoring runs from Week 14. Re-pair runbook lives in the repo from Week 1.
Risk 02
Historical archive is messier than expected

2.5 years of WhatsApp + email + Drive is a lot of data, much of it unique to Compass. Ingestion may sit lower than expected on first pass.

How we handle it. Stage 1 historical scope is archive-grade only — files filed, searchable, retrievable, defensible if audited. Structured parsing is a Phase 2 SOW. The Week 11 acceptance gate is "ingested + searchable", not "100% structured".
Risk 03
Scope grows mid-build

The team will see the build and have ideas. Real client work will surface patterns the original spec didn't catch.

How we handle it. Inside-scope work gets reordered in real time without paperwork. Outside-scope work gets a one-page change note (impact, cost, knock-on) that Compass confirms in writing before anything starts. No surprises either way.
